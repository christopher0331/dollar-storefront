# The $1 AI Prompt Pack
### 25 Copy-Paste-Ready Prompts for Business & Productivity

*Tested, structured, and ready to drop into ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any LLM. Each prompt is self-contained — just replace the `[bracketed]` placeholders.*

---

## 1. Writing & Content
**1. Blog Post Outline**
> You are a senior content strategist. Write a detailed blog post outline about [TOPIC] for an audience of [AUDIENCE]. Include an SEO-optimized title, 5-7 H2 sections with bullet sub-points, a hook intro, and a CTA. Tone: [TONE].

**2. Rewrite for Clarity**
> Rewrite the following text to be 40% shorter and easier to understand for a non-expert. Keep all key facts. Preserve the original meaning. Text: "[PASTE TEXT]"

**3. Newsletter Draft**
> Act as an email marketer. Draft a 200-word newsletter about [SUBJECT] with a compelling subject line, one story, one tip, and a soft CTA. Brand voice: [VOICE].

**4. Social Caption Pack**
> Generate 10 engaging Instagram/Twitter captions for [PRODUCT] targeting [AUDIENCE]. Mix educational, humorous, and promotional. Include 3 relevant hashtags each.

**5. Cold Email**
> Write a 90-word cold email to [PERSON/ROLE] at [COMPANY] offering [OFFER]. No fluff, one clear ask, personalized opener based on their recent [ACHIEVEMENT/POST].

---

## 2. Marketing & Sales
**6. UVP Generator**
> Help me define a unique value proposition for [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Target customer: [CUSTOMER]. Format: one sentence + 3 supporting bullet points. Avoid jargon.

**7. Competitor Analysis**
> Analyze [COMPETITOR] as if you are a product strategist. Cover: positioning, pricing, strengths, weaknesses, and one gap we could exploit. Be specific.

**8. Landing Page Copy**
> Write landing page copy for [PRODUCT]: headline, subheadline, 3 benefit bullets, objection-handling FAQ (3 Qs), and a CTA button label. Audience: [AUDIENCE].

**9. Pricing Strategy**
> Suggest 3 pricing tiers for [PRODUCT] aimed at [MARKET]. For each, give price, what's included, and the psychological rationale. Note which tier drives the most upgrades.

**10. Objection Handler**
> List the 8 most common objections buyers give for [PRODUCT TYPE] and a crisp 1-sentence rebuttal for each.

---

## 3. Productivity & Operations
**11. Daily Plan**
> Given my goals [GOALS] and available 4 focused hours today, build a prioritized schedule with time blocks, breaks, and one "if nothing else, do this" task.

**12. Meeting Agenda**
> Create a 30-minute meeting agenda for [TOPIC] with 4 agenda items, owner per item, and desired outcome. Keep it tight.

**13. Email Triage**
> Classify these emails into: Urgent/Today, This Week, Someday, Archive. For urgent ones, suggest a 1-line reply. Emails: "[PASTE LIST]"

**14. SOP Writer**
> Write a step-by-step standard operating procedure for [TASK]. Include prerequisites, numbered steps, expected output, and common mistakes to avoid.

**15. Decision Framework**
> I must choose between [OPTION A] and [OPTION B] for [CONTEXT]. Build a weighted decision matrix with 5 criteria, scores 1-5, and a recommendation.

---

## 4. Coding & Technical
**16. Code Explainer**
> Explain what this code does line-by-line for a junior dev. Then suggest 2 improvements. Code: "[PASTE CODE]"

**17. Bug Fixer**
> This code throws [ERROR]. Find the bug, explain the root cause, and provide a corrected version. Code: "[PASTE CODE]"

**18. Regex Builder**
> Write a regex that matches [DESCRIPTION, e.g. valid US phone numbers]. Explain each part. Provide test cases (3 positive, 3 negative).

**19. SQL Query**
> Write a SQL query for [DATABASE CONTEXT] that [GOAL, e.g. finds top 10 customers by revenue last 30 days]. Optimize for readability.

**20. API Spec**
> Design a REST API endpoint for [RESOURCE] with method, path, request body, response schema (JSON), and 2 error cases. Keep it pragmatic.

---

## 5. Strategy & Learning
**21. SWOT**
> Produce a SWOT analysis for [BUSINESS/IDEA] in [MARKET]. 4 quadrants, 4 bullets each, with one actionable next step per quadrant.

**22. 30-Day Plan**
> Create a 30-day launch plan for [PRODUCT] with weekly milestones, key tasks, and a metric to hit each week. Solo founder, limited budget.

**23. Explain Like I'm 5**
> Explain [CONCEPT] to a 5-year-old using an analogy, then to a smart 15-year-old, then to a professional. Three versions.

**24. Book Summary**
> Summarize the key ideas of [BOOK/TOPIC] in 5 bullet points with one practical action for each. Under 250 words total.

**25. Interview Questions**
> Generate 10 insightful interview questions for hiring a [ROLE], split into behavioral (5) and technical (5), with what a strong answer looks like for each.
